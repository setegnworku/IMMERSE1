The Challenge

Africa’s Quadruple Livestock Crisis

Enteric methane costs farmers 3–12% of feed energy while methane overall contributes nearly 30% of current global warming. African livestock systems face four interconnected challenges: low productivity, high methane intensity, weak monitoring systems, and limited scalable mitigation solutions — demanding an integrated response.

High Enteric Methane

Enteric fermentation is a major contributor to agricultural GHG emissions across Africa — and a direct loss of productive energy for animals.

Low Productivity

Seasonal feed scarcity, poor forage quality, and sub-optimal rations constrain intake, digestibility, milk yield, and farmer income.

Reproductive Inefficiency

Heat detection accuracy of only 50–70% and calving intervals of 18–24 months drastically reduce lifetime milk yield and herd profitability.

Limited Tools

Most validated mitigation technologies remain inaccessible to African smallholders. Affordable methane measurement systems are scarce.

IMMRSE-AFRICA: The Integrated Answer

A continentally coordinated platform — evolved from the original IMMeRSE regional initiative following World Bank Group guidance — that combines feed innovation, probiotic technology, affordable CH₄ measurement tools, drone-based MRV, circular bio-economy, and AI-driven analytics into a single, scalable system.

KalPro™ Gen-II: Next-Generation Methane-Oxidizing Probiotic

Incorporating methanotrophs from African rice paddies that actively oxidize methane via MMO enzymes. Co-formulated with existing KalPro™ consortium and nutritional seaweed supplements for synergistic effects — reducing emissions while improving drought resilience and rumen health.

Methodology

Multi-Tiered R&D Pipeline

From laboratory screening through controlled trials to continent-wide on-farm validation — a rigorous, phased approach ensuring only proven solutions reach farmers.

1

In Vitro Screening

Batch fermentation & 12-day bioreactors. Dose-response for CH₄, VFAs, digestibility.

2

On-Station Trials
